# ADR 008: Co-responsibility Governance Architecture

## Status
Accepted

## Context
Standard AI automation in finance often relies on "Human-in-the-Loop" (HITL) workflows, which can lead to "rubber-stamping" where humans blindly approve AI suggestions. In decentralized or multinational finance, this creates a vacuum of accountability and increases the risk of corruption or embezzlement via manual reclassification.

## Decision
We implement a **Co-responsibility Architecture (CRA)**. Kontablo nodes are defined by deterministic logical boundaries. While the human accountant remains the final authority ("AI proposes, Human disposes"), the system enforces a digital witness mechanism.

### Key Components:
1.  **Deterministic Boundaries:** Every Kontablo node (Level 3) must satisfy a binary inclusion/exclusion criteria (e.g., Physical vs. Digital money).
2.  **Inconsistency Flagging:** If a human user maps a local account to a Kontablo node while violating that node's deterministic logic, the system triggers an `inconsistency_flag`.
3.  **Audit Persistence:** The flag and an associated `inconsistency_note` (generated by the AI auditor) are stored immutablemente in the transaction's metadata.

## Consequences
- **Positive:** Reduces institutional corruption by ensuring that any "illogical" accounting manual entry is permanently flagged for auditors.
- **Positive:** Operationalizes AI shared responsibility models (ISACA/EU AI Act) at the ledger level.
- **Negative:** Requires continuous maintenance of the "Deterministic Boundary Library" as new financial instruments emerge.
- **Negative:** Increased metadata storage requirements for every mapping response.
