\begin{abstract}
\textbf{Kontablo}\footnote{\textbf{Etymology.} \emph{Kontablo} is a neologism coined for this project. Its roots are Esperanto: \emph{konto} (a financial account) and the cognate \emph{kontado} (accountancy), both tied to \emph{kalkuli} (to calculate, to reckon) (Reta Vortaro, \url{https://vortaro.net}). The form deliberately echoes the Romance \emph{contable}/\emph{contabile} (``accountant'', ``of accounts''), making the term immediately recognizable to Spanish-, Portuguese-, Italian-, and French-speaking readers while remaining neutral in spirit. We read \emph{Kontablo} as ``a system of accounts'': a sustained, calculable accounting discipline rather than a single ledger --- fitting for a universal standard built for a hybrid economy of human and AI agents. (Morphology note: regular Esperanto accountancy derivations use \emph{-ado}/\emph{-eco}; \emph{Kontablo} is a deliberate coinage, not a standard inflection.)} is an open, graph-based universal accounting ontology anchored to International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS) and empirically validated across 195 sovereign jurisdictions (complete global coverage), providing a deterministic, jurisdiction-agnostic subledger for both multinational ERP consolidation and the Machine-to-Machine (M2M) Agentic Economy.

The global accounting ecosystem remains fragmented across incompatible national standards --- an ``Accounting Babel'' that costs multinational enterprises billions in manual reconciliation and poses a structural barrier to high-frequency autonomous transactions. As AI agents begin to execute financial operations via Agent Payments (AP2) and Agent2Agent (A2A) protocols, they require a semantic execution layer that existing reporting standards (XBRL) and payment messaging protocols (ISO 20022) do not provide.

Kontablo addresses this gap through three core contributions: (1)~a Level~3 minimum-core taxonomy of 30 universal accounts empirically covering 92\% of routine transaction volume across all tested jurisdictions; (2)~a \textbf{Three-Tier Resolution Strategy} combining deterministic exact-code lookups and regex-based disambiguation rules with a confidence-scored semantic AI fallback, bounded by a Deterministic Boundary Library that eliminates the classical accounting hallucination class at the harness level; and (3)~a \textbf{Co-responsibility Architecture (CRA)} that pairs every AI mapping proposal with a mandatory human review pathway and an immutable inconsistency audit trail, ensuring legal accountability rests with the human operator in all cases. We validate the framework using a mass-consolidation engine across 10 entities in 9 countries, including a dual-currency hyperinflation stress test under IAS~29 (Venezuela). Kontablo is published under the Business Source License~1.1, converting to Apache~2.0 after four years.
\end{abstract}
